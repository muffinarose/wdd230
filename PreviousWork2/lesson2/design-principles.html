Repetition

Best Buy

https://www.bestbuy.com/

The home page of the Best Buy website features a simple grid of repeating circles with images in them. Each circle represents a specific category and is a clickable link that directs to that category's page. Circles are repeated throughout the web site used for many other things, including subcategories, customer service, and the social media links in the site footer.

White Space and Clean Design

Apple

https://www.apple.com/

Apple is well known for their beautiful, simple designs. Their web site is no exception. They use striking photographs, limited text, and simple fonts. All of this is surrounded by ample negative space (in this case, the “white space” is not always white) which highlights and compliments the featured products.

Hick's Law

FedEx

https://www.fedex.com/

FedEx has simplified their home page so that visitors can quickly and easily do what they are most likely there for, eliminating too much choice. Right at the top there are 3 main buttons for the three most likely reasons a user would visit: shipping, tracking, and finding a store. In addition to this, there is another big, bright button right in the middle that is also for tracking. Other options can be found in the peripheries, but don't distract from the three main options.
